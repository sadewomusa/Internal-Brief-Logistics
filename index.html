Truck Staging & Landside Hub at KCT
Physical Infrastructure — Fast Win
Missing▼
KCT has no organised truck staging zone. With Sajaa Rail confirmed and volumes increasing, long truck queues on public roads will worsen unless addressed now. This is the fastest buildable item on the list.
  • Gated bays (number to be determined) with ANPR cameras for auto-registration
  • Digital slot booking system — linked to GT digital platform
  • Driver amenities: prayer room, canteen, ablution, showers, rest area
  • Pre-clearance documentation window — Customs agent desk on-site
  • Fuel station (consider EV charging provision for future fleet)
  • Dangerous goods segregation bays
  • Reefer plug points for waiting refrigerated trucks

Cold Chain & Food Logistics Cluster
Specialist Product — High Differentiation
Underdeveloped▼
K-Flow has cold storage — but it is not yet positioned as a dedicated cold chain product. Fujairah lacks reefer plug capacity, making KCT the east coast's natural cold chain advantage. Current geopolitical climate amplifies the food security argument.

🌡️ Temperature Zones

Ambient / Chilled +2–8°C / Frozen -18°C / Pharma-grade -25°C. Each zone separately certified and monitored.

🔌 Reefer Infrastructure

Port-side reefer plug points — currently the east coast's biggest gap vs. Jebel Ali and Khalifa Port.

💊 Pharma GDP Cell

Dedicated GDP-compliant pharmaceutical storage licensed by Sharjah Health Authority. Separate air handling, continuous logging.

🥩 Halal Food Room

Bonded, sealed, separate air handling. Halal-certified food processing and repackaging room within K-Flow bonded zone.

Target Certifications
  • HACCP certification
  • ISO 22000 (Food Safety Management)
  • GDP (Good Distribution Practice) for pharma
  • IoT temperature logging with customer-facing compliance dashboard

Grade A Warehousing Park — Maleha / Sajaa
Planned Asset — Features & Anchor Strategy
In Planning▼
The park's location adjacent to the Etihad Rail station at Sajaa is a fundamental differentiator. No other industrial park in the Northern Emirates offers rail + port + bonded corridor access in one location. This must be the core pitch.
Grade A Physical Spec
  • Clear height: minimum 12m (ideally 14m for high-bay racking / ASRS compatibility)
  • Floor load: 5–7 tonnes/sqm (supports racking + forklift traffic)
  • Column spacing: 12m × 24m minimum (robotics-compatible floor layout)
  • ESFR sprinkler system (early suppression, fast response — required by 3PLs)
  • Dock door ratio: 1 per 800–1,000 sqm
  • FM2 floor flatness standard
  • LED lighting with motion sensors throughout
  • Solar panels on roof (UAE Net Zero 2050 + ESG tenant requirements)
  • EV charging in truck yard (10 units minimum to future-proof)
  • Fibre optic connectivity — 1Gbps minimum
  • RFID gantries at dock doors (auto-records every pallet in/out)
  • IoT sensors: temperature, humidity, movement — streamed to GT platform
  • CCTV with AI object detection and alerting
  • WMS pre-integrated — tenants plug SKUs, GT system manages inventory

🏢 Type A — Large BTS

10,000–50,000 sqm plots. Build-to-suit for 3PLs, multinationals. Long leases 5–15 years. Anchor tenant de-risks the park.

🏠 Type B — SME Units

500–3,000 sqm pre-built units. Short-term leases. Plug-and-play. Serves Northern Emirates manufacturers displaced from Dubai's full industrial zones.

Sajaa Dry Port — Rail Integration Specs
Design Requirements — Rail Station Confirmed
Rail Confirmed▼
Sajaa Dry Port must now be designed as a Rail-Road-Bonded Intermodal Hub — not simply a road-based ICD. The rail station changes the facility's design class entirely. Every spec below should be treated as a design requirement, not an aspiration.
  • Rail ramp / container loading dock integrated into the dry port footprint
  • Intermodal yard: dedicated lane separating rail-fed from truck-fed containers
  • Priority customs lane for rail cargo — mirror the Khalifa–Fujairah bonded corridor model
  • Bonded rail corridor designation — formal application through Sharjah Customs authority
  • Rail schedule integration: dry port gate ops aligned to Etihad Rail freight windows
  • ISPS Code compliance (International Ship & Port Facility Security)
  • 24/7 operations — must match KCT terminal hours
  • Automated gate: 6–10 lanes, ANPR, digital slot booking linked to GT platform
  • Customs office on-site (Sharjah Customs staffed, Mirsal 2 connected)
  • Reefer points in container yard (minimum to be determined)
  • Dangerous goods compound: segregated, fire-suppressed, IATA/IMDG compliant
  • Container repair yard
  • Weighbridges (minimum 2)
  • Bonded warehouse block within footprint
Discussion Questions

Products & Features to Define
🔗
The Sharjah Corridor — Named Product
Non-Infrastructure Gap — Zero Capex Required
Needs Definition▼
GT has all the physical assets for a world-class bonded corridor — but it is not a named, priced, or marketed product. This is the lowest-cost, highest-return gap to close. A commercial product can be launched in 3–6 months with no capex.
What the Product Must Include
  • A defined name (e.g. "Sharjah Corridor", "GT Corridor", "SharjahLink" — TBD)
  • A defined transit time SLA: KCT discharge → Sajaa Dry Port in a guaranteed window
  • A defined price per TEU for each corridor leg (road + rail options)
  • A single contract covering: port → K-Flow → corridor → Sajaa → storage → rail → delivery
  • A single invoice — not 4 separate GT/Momentum invoices
  • A rate card aligned to Etihad Rail freight schedule windows
  • A dedicated commercial owner at Momentum Logistics who sells it

Customer Journeys
3 Core Use Cases
To Validate▼
Journey 1 — "Import → Distribute to GCC via Rail"
Vessel @ KCT→K-Flow Bonded→Inland Corridor→Sajaa Dry Port→Rail→GCC→Priority Customs

Target: FMCG importers from Asia distributing to UAE, Saudi, Kuwait, Qatar. KCT avoids Jebel Ali congestion premium. Rail replaces expensive bonded trucking across UAE.

Journey 2 — "Northern Emirates Export via KCT"
Factory→Sajaa Stuffing→Container Sealed→Bonded Truck→KCT→Vessel Departs

Target: Sharjah, RAK, UAQ, Ajman manufacturers exporting to East Africa, India, Pakistan, Iraq. Full export cycle within GT ecosystem — Jebel Ali not required.

Journey 3 — "Abu Dhabi Importer Using KCT as Gateway"
Vessel→KCT→K-Flow Bonded→Corridor→Sajaa→Rail→Abu Dhabi Hub→Customs Release

Target: Abu Dhabi importers currently using Khalifa Port. Rail from Sajaa makes KCT a viable Abu Dhabi gateway — entirely new customer segment enabled by the Rail station.
